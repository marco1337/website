---
title: Writing Internal Tools People Actually Keep Using
description: A few habits that made the difference between a tool that gets adopted and one that gets abandoned after a month.
date: 2025-06-20
---

Internal tools die for boring reasons: they're slower than the workaround, they
break in ways nobody can debug, or the person who built them left. None of that
is about the tech stack.

The deployment dashboard I built early in my career is still in daily use years
later, and I think it's because it did one thing well, failed loudly instead of
silently, and had a five-minute onboarding. Scope was the feature.
